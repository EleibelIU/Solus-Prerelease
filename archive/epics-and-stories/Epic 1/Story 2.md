0Five ability scores (Body, Mind, Social, Magic, Sanity) contain numerical values from -5 to +5. 

Body
	This stat represents your over all physical prowess. 
		**Physical attacks & damage** – Melee, ranged, unarmed, weapon accuracy tied to bodily control
		**Defense & mobility** – Dodging, initiative, movement speed, physical saves
		**Durability** – Resistance to poison, disease, exhaustion, environmental harm

Mind
	This stat represents both your learned and worldly knowledge
		**Knowledge & skills** – Lore, investigation, crafting, medicine, tactics
		**Perception & awareness** – Noticing threats, insight, tracking, intuition
		**Mental saves (non-emotional)** – Resisting illusions, confusion, mind-affecting logic effects

Social
	This stat represents communicative prowess
		**Social influence** – Persuasion, deception, intimidation, performance
		**Leadership & morale** – Commanding allies, inspiring courage, rallying NPCs
		**Social defenses** – Resisting manipulation, charm, coercion

Magic
	This stat represents how magically adept you are.
		**Spellcasting effectiveness** – Accuracy, damage, scaling
		**Magical control** – Concentration

Sanity
	This stat represents your mental fortitude
		**Fear & madness saves** – Horror, terror, eldritch effects
		**Stress tolerance** – Long-term pressure, corruption, psychological damage    
		**Mental break thresholds** – Panic, trauma, loss of control states


### Combat
Combat rolls use 2d10(additive not percentile) + Stat Modifier vs Armor DR + Stat Modifier

A Critical Success is double 10s (10 +10= 20)
A Critical Failure is double 1s (1+1=2)

## Resist and Checks.

### Resist Rolls
To Resist and effect you roll 2d10(additive not percentile) + Stat Modifier against a gradient from -5/-10 to +5/+10. These will determine the different degrees of success and failure rather than a binary outcome.

Roll to resist 
Attribute-Based Resistance with Degrees of Outcome
- Each **Stat governs resistance** to a category of threats
- You **roll + modifier**
- The result is interpreted on a **gradient** (±5 / ±10), not pass/fail
- Success is **not always desirable**
- Failure is **not always negation**

### Skill Checks
When attempting to determine information or something actionable you roll an Skill Check.
An Skill Check is determined by 2d10(additive not percentile) and the associated Skill modifier. 

The Skill modifier is always based on 2 stats 1 Primary and a Secondary.

- The Primary stat: is the dominate stat that an ability belongs in, this stat is predetermined and cannot change. 
- The Secondary stat: is a supplemental stat that is added to the Primary, this additive stat is a choice between 2 others that the player choses.

### **Athletics** — _Primary: Body_

Overcome physical challenges through strength, endurance, speed, bodily control.

- **Secondary: Mind**
    _Why:_ Learned technique and bodily efficiency without becoming Survival or Investigation.  
    _Example:_ Using trained climbing form to conserve strength.
    
- **Secondary: Sanity**  
    _Why:_ Physical feats can fail due to panic or loss of control.  
    _Example:_ Clinging to a ledge while fear threatens to make you let go.
---

### **Stealth** — _Primary: Body_

Move, act, or remain unnoticed.

- **Secondary: Mind**  
    _Why:_ Awareness of timing, sightlines, and attention.  
    _Example:_ Moving only when a guard turns away.
    
- **Secondary: Sanity** 
    _Why:_ Remaining hidden despite fear or sudden danger.  
    _Example:_ Freezing in place instead of panicking when nearly spotted.
---

### **Investigation** — _Primary: Mind_

Search for, analyze, and piece together clues.

- **Secondary: Body**  
    _Why:_ Physical interaction during searching without becoming Athletics.  
    _Example:_ Shifting rubble to examine what’s underneath.
    
- **Secondary: Sanity**  
    _Why:_ Continuing analysis despite disturbing discoveries.  
    _Example:_ Examining a gruesome scene without shutting down.
---

### **Knowledge** — _Primary: Mind_

Recall, understand, or contextualize learned information.

- **Secondary: Social**
    _Why:_ Understanding cultural or social context without influencing others.  
    _Example:_ Knowing noble etiquette or historical alliances.
    
- **Secondary: Sanity**  
    _Why:_ Some knowledge is destabilizing or corruptive.  
    _Example:_ Recalling forbidden lore without suffering a mental break.
---

### **Medicine** — _Primary: Mind_

Assess health, diagnose, treat injuries, stabilize the wounded.

- **Secondary: Body**  
    _Why:_ Physical steadiness and endurance during treatment.  
    _Example:_ Performing surgery after hours of exhaustion.
    
- **Secondary: Sanity**  
    _Why:_ Treating traumatic injuries without freezing or dissociating.  
    _Example:_ Working calmly in a field hospital under pressure.
---

### **Survival** — _Primary: Mind_

Endure, navigate, track, hunt, or live off the land.

- **Secondary: Body**  
    _Why:_ Physical endurance and resistance to harsh environments.  
    _Example:_ Marching for days through extreme cold.
    
- **Secondary: Sanity**  
    _Why:_ Long-term isolation and sustained danger.  
    _Example:_ Remaining functional while lost alone for weeks.
---

### **Animal Handling** — _Primary: Social_

Calm, train, control, or intuit animals.

- **Secondary: Body**  
    _Why:_ Physical presence and controlled movement influence animals.  
    _Example:_ Standing firm to assert dominance over a dangerous beast.
    
- **Secondary: Mind**  
    _Why:_ Interpreting behavior and recognizing warning signs.  
    _Example:_ Noticing agitation before an animal bolts.
---

### **Performance** — _Primary: Social_

Entertain, inspire, distract, or convey emotion through expression.

- **Secondary: Body**  
    _Why:_ Physical execution and stamina are often required.  
    _Example:_ Sustaining an acrobatic dance routine.
    
- **Secondary: Sanity**  
    _Why:_ Performing under stress or hostile conditions.  
    _Example:_ Continuing despite a terrifying or hostile audience.
---

### **Speech** — _Primary: Social_

Persuade, deceive, negotiate, command, or influence through words.

- **Secondary: Body**  
    _Why:_ Physical presence reinforces persuasion or intimidation.  
    _Example:_ Using posture and controlled movement to make a threat convincing.
    
- **Secondary: Mind**  
    _Why:_ Reasoning, awareness, and adapting arguments.  
    _Example:_ Shifting tactics mid-negotiation.
---

### **Arcana** — _Primary: Magic_

Identify, understand, manipulate magical or eldritch forces.

- **Secondary: Mind**  
    _Why:_ Reasoning and structured understanding of magic.  
    _Example:_ Analyzing a spell’s structure.
    
- **Secondary: Sanity**  
    _Why:_ Eldritch forces risk madness or corruption.  
    _Example:_ Studying forbidden rituals without breaking.
---

### **Insight** — _Primary: Sanity_

Resist fear, terror, shock; avoid panic; act rationally under horror.

- **Secondary: Body**
    _Why:_ Preventing physical panic responses.  
    _Example:_ Keeping steady hands instead of trembling.
    
- **Secondary: Mind**  
    _Why:_ Rational thought supports emotional control.  
    _Example:_ Making clear decisions during extreme fear.
---
