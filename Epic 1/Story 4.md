NPCs come in 5 ranks Minion, Regular, Enemy/Ally , Mini Boss, and Boss.

They all use a modified version of a PC stat block based on their rank. The lower the rank the less health they have, the less actions, less power and equipment they have.

A Dm needs the Stat Block of the NPC which will have a modified version of PC characters in order to run this.

These NPCs interact with the Core Gameplay Loop by being apart of the World Problems, The Consequences and the Rewards. They could be the reason a problem happens or persists. They could be the consequences for actions that PCs have made as friend or foe. They can be apart of the reward by being the ones to distribute said reward after successfully or unsuccessfully, completing the consequences loop.

All NPCs use the same action economy, equipment, skills, and spell systems as PCs.

Bosses and Minions are exceptions to the rules, see below for more.
### Minion
Minions are meant to have 1-5 HP, they are easy to kill and will do little damage. By themselves they are not a threat however the more there is the more dangerous they become. These enemies are a good way to create swarms or hordes since they will die quickly. 

They will always have 0s in every Stat, this is meant to reflect the low threat they carry as an individual. They will break the concept of "using the same as PCs" but simply by being an edge case on the low end.

### Enemy/Ally
These NPCs are the main and most common of enemies. They are meant to be almost a mirrored version of the PCs to give them a challenge as they are meant to be competent people. Their stat block should have only slight differences like total HP, equipment, and skills but otherwise should reflect a proper challenge.

Allies are meant to operate in a similar manner, but to help and not hinder PCs.

### Boss
This NPC should have a similar stat block to a PC but much further along with more skills, deeper investment in skills, more health, better equipment and similar. These are meant to present a real challenge to PCs and demand sound tactics and good planning.

The Boss also breaks the norm for NPC Stat block building. Here they have one more +5 than is allowed by the point buy system and this is by design. This is meant to reflect a true challenge to even the most well equipped and prepared party. By giving them this extra Stat increase it allows what could have been a normal NPC to become a monstrous threat with simply one change and one change only.
