## Dropping to 0 HP
*Yes I need to figure this out and am still working on it and what might be the best solution. So I will put a proposal here for you to look at and give me your thoughts on it all and how it sounds and feels to you.*

When your HP reaches 0, you are in dying state. 
While in the dying state you gain a countdown starting at 10 (tracked with a d10 or other marker).
When your countdown reaches 0, you die.

You remain conscious and may act normally, with the following penalties:
- Any attack roll is made with disadvantage and your movement is halved.
- Your mana regen rate is half rounded down.

You have 3 actions on your turn. Each unused action counts as ‘do nothing’ and reduces the countdown by 1
For every action you choose to spend by doing something the countdown will go down by 2 for each action.
The countdown is reduced immediately after each action (yours or an enemy’s) resolves per successful attack.
You can mix this in any fashion so long as you have actions to take.

For example.

Round 1 
You enter the dying state and start your countdown at 10.

Round 2
Action 1 you choose to move, once completed the countdown goes down by 2 bringing you to 8.
Action 2 you choose to do nothing and the countdown goes down by 1 bringing you to 7.
Action 3 you choose to do nothing and the countdown goes down by 1 bringing you to 6.

- If an attacker hits you with an attack using 1 action reduce your countdown by 1.
- If an attack that required 2 actions hits you, reduce your countdown by 3.
- If an attacker hits you with an attack using 3 actions reduce your countdown by 5. 
- If reduced to 0, you die immediately.

For example
If you start at 10 and get hit
1 action places you at 9
2 actions places you at 7
3 actions places you at 5

If you get healed for 1 HP you leave the dying state.
If you remain above 0 HP until the start of your next turn, your countdown resets to 10
If you return to 0 HP before your countdown resets, resume the previous countdown value instead of starting at 10.

**Optional Rule (Swingy Variant)** 
For a deadlier and less predictable experience, use the following instead:
 - If an attacker hits you with a 1-action attack, reduce your countdown by 1.

 - If an attacker hits you with a 3-action attack, reduce your countdown to 1.
