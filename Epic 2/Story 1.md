# Degree of 5
Social and Situational mechanics will operate in degrees of 5 as listed in the table below.
A Difficulty Class (DC) will be set based on how challenging the situation or the desired outcome.
Some DC may be very low such as a DC of 5, to average DC 10, to very High DC 20 or more.
Characters are considered to be competent and are expected to operate well in situations familiar and either poorly in situations unfamiliar, with this a roll is only needed when an outcome may be uncertain due to a desired outcome, situation, or circumstances a character might find themselves in.

Here are 2 scenarios involving the same character, a general adventurer

Scenario 1:
The adventurer might be good at exploring dungeons and would be expected to have knowledge that is common to all and how to act in tough situations like searching for a trap. In these cases a roll is not necessary, but if a trap is particularly dangerous or not the standard then a roll might be required, at this point a DC is set based on the difficult of the trap. 
Lets say the DC for this trap is a 15, so DC 15, but the character has experience dealing with traps before so the DC could be lowered by 5 making it a DC of 10. 
This is meant to reflect the knowledge and skills that a character has gained throughout their time dungeon diving. 

Scenario 2:
On the other end however this same adventurer might be from a small town or village and not know how to properly great a noble or how to navigate a big city. In these cases when trying to navigate a big city or properly great a noble and they want to see if they can recall any information they might have seen or heard a DC may be set. While the situation they are in may not be hard to deal with their lack of knowledge places them at a disadvantage. 
Lets say the DC for this is a 10, but with their lack of knowledge it rases the difficulty by 5 making it a DC of 15.
This is meant to reflect the lack of knowledge or skill set to complete this task.

In both cases you can see how a DC may or may not be needed depending on the situation and how the DC might be augmented based on the Character themselves. 
To be clear a DC can be raised or lowered by any number typically between 0-10.

| Degree  | Result                              | Outcome                                                            |
| ------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| + 10    | Beat DC by 10 or more               | Gain a positive result and a big bonus                             |
| + 5     | Beat DC by 5 or more                | Gain a positive result and a small bonus                           |
| +/- 0-4 | Meet/Beat/Below the given DC by 0-4 | Expected outcome either succeeds or fails with no additive outcome |
| - 5     | Below DC by 5 or more               | Gain a negative result and a small setback                         |
| - 10    | Below DC by 10 or more              | Gain a positive result and a big setback                           |

Now with the chart logic applied let relook at both scenarios and how the end result for each might play out.

Scenario 1:
Original DC is 15 but with experience and competency the DC is lowered to 10.

| Degree  | Outcome                                                                      |
| ------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| + 10    | Adventurer finds, disarms and can take the parts to recreate the trap        |
| + 5     | Adventurer finds trap and disarms it                                         |
| +/- 0-4 | Adventurer finds trap before activating it/ Adventurer sets the trap off     |
| - 5     | Adventurer sets trap off and damages the surrounding area                    |
| - 10    | Adventurer sets trap off, damages surroundings, and loses function of a limb |

Scenario 2:
Original DC 10 but with a lack of knowledge or experience DC is raised to 15

| Degree  | Outcome                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| ------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| + 10    | Adventurer has a great sense cutting travel time in half and finds interesting POIs or greats the noble properly, impressing them to the point the noble is interested in the Adventurer and willing to do them a favor         |
| + 5     | Adventurer has a general sense of surroundings and makes good time or greats the noble properly and impressing them making conversation easy                                                                                    |
| +/- 0-4 | Adventurer gets a general sense of surroundings, or greats noble properly/ Adventurer does not get a sense of surroundings or greats the noble improperly                                                                       |
| - 5     | Adventurer gets makes mistakes and has to double back to find way or greats the noble poorly upsetting them making conversation a bit difficult                                                                                 |
| - 10    | Adventurer is helplessly lost and every turn is a dead end, they will need help to find their way or greats the noble poorly and makes offense causing the noble to walk away refusing to talk and possibly contacting security |

While this may not always be the way things play out, in both cases you can see examples of different outcomes based on several factors all while using the Degree of 5 system. 
It should also be noted that you can always shift failure to still be a success but now has a negative consequence.
Lets use the Noble example for this.

| Degree | Outcome                                                                                                                                                      |
| ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| - 5    | Adventurer greats the noble properly but poorly making the noble standoffish and conversation difficult                                                      |
| - 10   | Adventurer greats the noble properly but poorly and makes offense causing the noble to have security monitor them and making conversation a tense situation. |
